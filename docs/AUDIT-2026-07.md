# Sawiyya — Full Product Audit (2026-07)

Synthesised from 10 dimension audits: engine-truth, learning-loop, curriculum, family-mode, pwa-mobile, a11y, state-resilience, code-health, funnel, business-readiness. Findings are deduplicated across dimensions and ranked by severity. Everything here was verified against the repo at `~/Documents/GitHub/sawiyya`, the landing source at `~/Desktop/Projects/sawiyya-landing`, and live PostHog data.

---

## Executive summary

The engine is real and the copy is not — that is the single most important truth of this audit. The 28-letter recognition stack (Zenodo ArSL seeds, calibrated MLP at 98.7% test accuracy, KNN fallback, honest tooling and docs) checks out end to end, but the product wrapped around it fabricates exactly the things it pledges never to fabricate: an in-app **"Mada Innovation Award Winner"** badge for an award that has not been judged, an About screen claiming every sign was **"taught, checked and approved by Deaf Qatari signers"** (no signer has been engaged), 16 word signs that are recognisably **ASL shipped as Qatari Sign Language**, an AI-generated image captioned "A Deaf signer demonstrates", **invented testimonials** on the landing page, and a Progress screen showing a **hardcoded fake family** (Mama/Layla/Sara/Baba) and fake stats to every user. Any Mada judge or Deaf QSL expert finds at least one of these within two minutes, and the ethics-led pitch dies with it — yet almost all of these are hours of copy/rebind work, not builds.

Second truth: the learning loop is structurally broken in ways no amount of polish hides. A routing bug makes Lesson 1 **impossible to complete forever** — the 827-line LessonPlayer, all quizzes, the review pseudo-lesson, and 12 of 16 word signs are dead code in the shipped flow; the SRS never receives a single failure rating, so it cannot adapt; the review queue both floods after a break and starves by day 10. Third: Family Mode, the differentiator, is half-wired — flagging a sign puts it in **nobody's queue**, tapping a flagged sign usually opens practice for the wrong sign (Alif), and the whole household lives in one browser's localStorage with no export and no disclosure. Fourth: infrastructure has cheap, high-value gaps — the service worker forces ~14MB of unused design PNGs onto every visitor, deploys ship to production with no test gate, there is no error boundary, no persist migration, and the landing page's analytics token points at nothing (zero events since June 23). Accessibility is above-average at baseline but fails WCAG contrast on the primary button and has zero focus management — a real liability for an accessibility-award entry. The good news is genuine: the recognizer numbers are honest and reproducible, the PWA fundamentals are solid, the codebase is small, strict and testable, and the fix list is dominated by S-effort items. The order of work is dictated by the pitch itself: tell the truth first, make the loop work second, make the differentiators real third, polish last.

## Per-dimension assessments

**Engine truth.** The recognition engine's numbers are real and internally consistent: MODEL_TAU, the model metadata (98.66% test acc, TA 98.21%, FA 0.05%), KNN gates, the 28-class seed set, MLP-primary/KNN-fallback wiring and the extract→train→calibrate tooling all check out, and the engine docs are unusually honest about caveats and the AASL NC-license block. The credibility failures are in the content layer on top: a false Deaf-signer approval claim and ASL descriptions shipped as QSL. The two real technical risks are the unverified left/right mirror convention (owner-gated live test) and the MLP's missing out-of-distribution gate; the rest is minor doc-vs-code drift.

**Learning loop.** FSRS wiring is competent, but the loop around it is structurally broken: a routing bug deadlocks the path on Lesson 1 forever, making the entire lesson engine dead code; every reachable drill rates 'good' so the scheduler never sees a failure and never adapts; the review queue floods after a break with no way to drain it, then starves by day 10 with no new-content injection. Streak/XP logic is mostly honest, with a stale-streak display and 60-second mastery farming as secondary issues.

**Curriculum.** The inventory is 47 items: 28 genuinely-graded letters (the real asset) plus 16 A1 words of which at least 10 are recognisably ASL with no QSL source — colliding with the never-fabricate non-negotiable. There is one unit, three lessons, no numbers, no Unit 2, and no fingerspelling mode despite every building block existing. The alphabet — the only content with a real trained model — has no lesson, ordering or milestone structure, while the unsourced words own the entire path; in practice the shipped app is a 32-item camera drill.

**Family Mode.** Mechanically real but only half the pitched loop exists: flags persist, pin, and badge — but never seed anyone's SRS queue, 12 of 16 flaggable signs deep-link to the wrong practice target, the assigner has zero progress visibility, "only the Deaf member flags" is unenforced (anyone can wipe the curriculum), and everything lives in one browser with no export. Worst for a judged entry, the Progress league renders four hardcoded fake family members while real profiles sit in the store. The saving grace: the data shapes already support the full loop — fixes are UI/store-level, mostly S/M.

**PWA & mobile.** Fundamentals are genuinely solid — valid manifest, autoUpdate SW, offline SPA fallback, correct touch targets and safe-area handling. The two real problems are payload (the SW precaches ~14MB of unused design PNGs; one 869KB JS chunk with a 621KB seed file inlined) and CDN dependency (the entire recognizer needs jsdelivr + Google storage at first camera use despite the wasm shipping locally). Committed dist/ is vestigial and stale, and iOS polish gaps (apple-touch-icon, pinch-zoom) matter for a Qatar-heavy iOS audience.

**Accessibility.** A strong indie baseline — global focus ring, reduced-motion clamps, an aria-live camera region, logical properties, pre-React dir/lang, Eastern-Arabic digit helpers — but not yet exemplary for a Deaf-inclusion product: the palette fails WCAG AA on the primary button and nav labels, there is zero focus management anywhere (keyboard users can be trapped), landmarks/headings are broken, emoji hands leak back in as sign representations, and one confirmed RTL regression (a non-existent Tailwind class) mispositions a camera badge in the live build.

**State resilience.** Small and mostly disciplined: single persisted store, versioned KNN store with quota fallback, per-screen guards, NaN-clamped primitives, honest camera-denied UI, and a safe legacy-data purge. The gaps are structural and forward-looking: no persist version/migrate (the next schema change breaks live users), no error boundary (one throw white-screens the app), corrupt-blob recovery is a silent total wipe, both stores are last-write-wins across tabs, and the detect loop freezes silently on an exception.

**Code health.** The core is in good shape — tsc-strict, 23/23 tests green, no debris in the source, every dependency used, all screens reachable. But the repo carries heavy post-rebuild residue: ~12.7MB of unreferenced brand PNGs the SW pushes onto every device, a stale committed dist/, two coexisting design systems, an orphaned 315-line component, and 39% dead i18n keys. The one significant infrastructure hole: deploys ship to production with no test gate, and all tests cover only the recognizer.

**Funnel.** Core mechanics beat average — correct CTA targets, verifiably analytics-free app, in-context camera permission, useful return-visit surfaces. But the top of the funnel promises what the product can't deliver (native-signer demos, "full course live now", an ILY hero the app doesn't serve, invented testimonials), measurement is dead (wrong PostHog token, zero events since June 23, "Notify me" captures nothing), day-2 return rests on a fake reminders step, and the Arabic-first journey breaks at the landing→app handoff. (Its "no PWA" finding was incorrect — see the conflict note above.)

**Business readiness.** The technical credibility story is genuinely strong — real Zenodo attribution, build stamp, calibrated thresholds, hidden pitch metrics, offline PWA backing the on-device claim, ethics visible in-app. But the shipped copy repeatedly fabricates exactly what the product pledges never to fabricate (award badge, signer approval, AI image as Deaf signer, ASL-as-QSL, testimonials, fake stats/league); any judge finds one within two minutes. Demo-day robustness is thin (camera-blocked dead-end; designed fallbacks never wired) and institutional surface is zero. Most fixes are hours of copy/wiring, not builds — the exceptions are the Deaf-signer content and any classroom surface.
